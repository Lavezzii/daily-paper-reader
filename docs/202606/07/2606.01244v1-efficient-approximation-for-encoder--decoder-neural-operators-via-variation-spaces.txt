# Efficient Approximation for Encoder–Decoder Neural Operators via Variation Spaces 

## Jia-Qi Yang and Lei Shi ∗

## School of Mathematical Sciences and Shanghai Key Laboratory for Contemporary Applied Mathematics, Fudan University, Shanghai 200433, China. 

Abstract 

We study operator learning using encoder–decoder neural networks. Inspired by the function-space theory of neural networks, we introduce a variation space as an infinite-dimensional structural class for nonlinear operators. This space is defined through vector-valued measures directly on the input and output spaces. For operators in this space, we establish approximation bounds for encoder–decoder two-layer networks in the Bochner Lq norm. The resulting error bound decom-poses into the input encoding error, the output encoding error, and a finite-width approximation term of order N −1/2, with a constant independent of the input and output encoding dimensions. When the input and output encoding errors decay polynomially in the encoding dimensions, these estimates yield algebraic approximation and learning rates. The results provide an theoretical guarantees for efficient neural operator learning beyond general Lipschitz or Fr´ echet differentiable operator classes. 

# 1 Introduction 

Operator learning aims to approximate operators whose inputs and outputs are functions rather than finite dimensional vectors. A prototypical example is the solution operator of a partial differential equation [24, 28], which maps coefficients, source terms, initial data, or boundary data to the cor-responding solution field. Other examples include evolution maps in dynamical systems [41], inverse maps for recovering unknown parameters [29], and control-to-state maps in optimal control [14]. The learned operator can then be evaluated at new input data without repeatedly solving the underlying model. Neural operators adapt neural network architectures to operator learning. One representative design incorporates learned integral operators into hidden layers, allowing the representation to capture nonlocal dependence across the domain. This structure appears in graph neural operator [4] and Fourier neural operator (FNO) [24], where the integral operator is discretized on graphs or parameterized in Fourier space, respectively. A distinct line of work is based on encoder-decoder architectures. In this approach, the input function is encoded into a finite dimensional vector, mapped by a neural network to finite dimensional output coordinates, and then decoded into the output function space. Representative examples include deep operator network (DeepONet) [28], motivated by earlier universal approximation results for continuous operators [9], and PCA-Net [7], where principal components are used for encoding and reconstruction. More generally, encoders and decoders may be defined using Fourier [12], Legendre polynomial [39], or wavelet basis expansions [13]. The approximation and generalization properties of neural operators have been extensively studied [18] in recent years. Universal approximation means that continuous operators can be approximated

For solution operators associ-ated with Darcy flow and the Navier–Stokes equations, quantitative approximation [17, 22, 21] results show that neural operator architectures can achieve accuracy ϵ with the number of neural network parameters bounded by a polynomial in ϵ−1. In contrast, for classes of k-times Fr´ echet differentiable operators, such polynomial parameter bounds generally fail for PCA-Net [21]. Moreover, lower bounds for general Lipschitz and Fr´ echet differentiable operator classes reveal curse of dimensionality phenom-ena in operator learning, both in parametric complexity [23] and, through n-width estimates, in data complexity [19]. Generalization theory studies how the error of a learned operator depends on the number of train-ing samples. In a general encoder–decoder framework, nonasymptotic generalization error bounds are available for empirical risk minimizers over neural network classes when the target operator is Lips-chitz continuous or exhibits a low dimensional structure [26]. For DeepONet, existing error estimates address generalization in nonlinear operator learning [22], while neural scaling laws quantify the be-havior for Lipschitz continuous target operators [27]. For Fr´ echet differentiable operators, prespecified encoder–decoder architectures yield approximation and generalization error bounds with improved rates [10] compared with those for Lipschitz continuous operators. Moreover, for Lipschitz operators, minimax lower bounds show that the minimax risk cannot decay polynomially with the sample size [2]. In Gaussian measure settings, sample complexity lower bounds preclude algebraic convergence over Lipschitz and Gaussian Sobolev operator classes for any recovery strategy based on finitely many, possibly adaptive, linear measurements [1]. These results suggest that efficient operator learning over very broad classes of nonlinear operators requires additional structural assumptions. A natural question is therefore to identify operator classes for which neural operators admit algebraic approximation and generalization rates. One possible direction is provided by holomorphic operator classes [35, 33], for which statistical learning theory for neural operators has been developed. A further related direction is based on the limiting neural tangent kernel (NTK), which extends the NTK framework to encoder–decoder architectures. Algebraic approximation rates for DeepONet have also been obtained for Lipschitz operators [34], but it rely on super-expressive activations or nonstandard neural network architectures. Although outside the neural-operator framework considered here, nonparametric approaches also offer routes to efficient operator learning: algebraic rates are available for learning linear operators in Hilbert spaces [36], while operator-valued kernel methods yield dimension-independent rates for nonlinear operator learning [43, 42]. A fruitful perspective is provided by the function-space theory of neural networks. In finite-dimensional approximation, structural spaces such as spectral Barron spaces and variation spaces have been used to characterize functions that can be efficiently represented by neural networks. Spec-tral Barron spaces [37, 25] originate from Barron’s work [6] and impose weighted L1 conditions on the Fourier transform, while variation spaces [5, 38] represent two-layer neural networks through fi-nite measures over the parameter space. Other relevant structural classes include Radon–BV spaces [31, 32] and Korobov spaces [30]. Motivated by this viewpoint, we develop a variation space for op-erator learning, where both the input and output spaces may be infinite dimensional. This space is defined directly for operators and is compatible with encoder–decoder neural network architectures. We establish approximation and generalization bounds for encoder–decoder neural networks for op-erators in variation space, showing that this structural class supports efficient learning whenever the associated input and output encoding errors decay polynomially with the encoding dimensions. A closely related work is [16], where vector-valued variation spaces are developed for two-layer neural networks between Banach spaces. That abstract framework, however, relies on an ordered structure on the output space: the output space is required to be a Riesz space, and the activation is realized through the lattice positive part in the output space. The associated weak-* continuity condition on the lattice operation is restrictive and, in particular, fails for Lq ([0 , 1] d), 1 < q ≤ ∞ .Moreover, that work does not address architectures for operator learning. This construction extends the classical scalar-valued variation space to V-valued operators by encoding the output weights through finite V-valued measures. The resulting class is formulated directly on the Hilbert spaces U and V, and is independent of the encoder– decoder dimensions as well as of the particular orthonormal bases used to form finite representations.
