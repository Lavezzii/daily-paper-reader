# Time-Varying Parametric Channel Estimation With CP Decomposition Tensor Processing 

Enrique T. R. Pinto, Graduate Student Member, IEEE , Andr´ e L. F. de Almeida, Senior Member, IEEE ,and Markku Juntti, Fellow, IEEE 

Abstract —Integrated sensing and communications (ISAC) is a key use case for sixth-generation (6G) wireless systems, where parametric channel estimation (PCE) plays a central role in enabling sensing, localization, and channel equalization in high-mobility scenarios. However, PCE is typically more computa-tionally demanding than conventional channel estimation, which motivates the development of lower-complexity solutions. In this letter, we propose a fast PCE algorithm for time-varying and frequency-selective (TVFS) channels based on canonical polyadic (CP) decomposition and tensor processing, combined with ESPRIT-based initialization, component refinement, and exact line-search alternating coordinate descent. Two variants are presented: one for fully digital and another for hybrid receiver architectures. Numerical results show that the proposed method clearly outperforms a related CP-based baseline while achieving estimation performance close to a multiple-start SAGE benchmark at a substantially lower computational cost, with about one order of magnitude shorter execution time. 

Index Terms —ISAC, parametric channel estimation, CP de-composition, tensor processing 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

# TENSOR processing methods have garnered increasing in-terest in wireless communications because they preserve the multilinear structure naturally present in many signal mod-els [1], [2], [3], [4], [5]. In parallel, the emergence of sensing-oriented functionalities in future wireless standards, such as integrated sensing and communications (ISAC) for sixth gen-eration standard (6G), has intensified the demand for accurate sensing, localization, and channel inference techniques [6], [7]. Among these techniques, parametric channel estimation (PCE) plays a central role because it provides geometric information about the channel, including the number of specular paths as well as their angles of arrival (AoAs), angles of departure (AoDs), time of flights (ToFs), and Doppler frequencies [8], [9]. Despite this potential, most tensor-based approaches are not well suited to time-varying scenarios: they either neglect Doppler, assume approximately constant subframes, or impose restrictive pilot structures. Tensor-based methods have been successfully applied to blind multiuser equalization [2], space-time coding [3], co-

More specifically, [12] proposed a tensor-based compressive sensing framework for massive MIMO-orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) channel estimation by exploit-ing the multilinear sparse structure of time- and frequency-selective channels. In [13], Cao et al. proposed a hybrid-field channel estimation method based on the canonical polyadic (CP) decomposition. In [9], Wen et al. developed a CP- and tensor-estimation of signal parameters via rotational invariant techniques (ESPRIT)-based method for PCE and mapping with diffuse multipath. However, these methods do not explic-itly address Doppler estimation in the formulation considered here. Likewise, methods such as [14], [15] assume that the number of paths is known a priori , which is unrealistic in practice, and require a two-stage pilot transmission, thereby increasing estimation overhead. The method in [16] estimates most channel parameters, but it does not reuse previously estimated AoA and ToF information to improve subsequent AoD and Doppler estimation. The main appeal of tensor-based PCE lies in their com-putational efficiency. Once the CP components have been estimated, several subsequent computations can be carried out in parallel. This property is particularly attractive in large-scale antenna arrays and high-mobility scenarios, where the channel model dimension increases significantly and low-latency processing becomes essential. In addition, multilinear factorization offers a compact representation of the received data, which helps separate the contributions of the different channel parameters before the final refinement stage. This is in contrast with maximum likelihood (ML) methods based on expectation-maximization (EM) [17] such as space-alternating generalized expectation-maximization (SAGE) [18], [19], [20] and Richter’s maximum likelihood estimation (RiMAX) [21], which are typically more sequential and usually have more computationally demanding parameter updates. In this letter, we address the gap above by proposing a single-stage tensor-based PCE procedure that explicitly estimates Doppler with low computational complexity. The proposed method, named component refinement with exact line search CP (CREL-CP), combines CP decomposition, tensor ESPRIT, component refinement, and exact line-search alternating coordinate descent (ACD) in a unified estimation pipeline. Its originality lies in coupling a low-complexity multilinear factorization stage with refinement steps that reuse intermediate geometric information to improve the final pa-

V. A LGORITHM COMPLEXITY 

We now analyze the computational complexity of the pro-posed method for the fully digital receiver case; for the hybrid combiner case, the same reasoning applies after replacing NR

with DR. In both the single-stream and multi-stream settings, the algorithm begins with model-order estimation followed by the CP decomposition. Model-order estimation requires com-puting three singular value decompositions (SVDs), with com-plexity Opmax tNC, N S, N Ru ¨ Nxq, where Nx “ NCNSNR.This first stage is not merely a preprocessing step: it deter-mines the effective tensor rank used in the subsequent multi-linear factorization and therefore directly influences the overall cost of the procedure. In this work, the CP decomposition is computed using the Gauss-Newton with dogleg trust region (GN-DL) algorithm, which has per-iteration complexity of 

Op ˆL3pNC `NS `NRq3q (given in flops /iteration) [26]. The remaining computations can be separated into ˆL in-dependent branches, each associated with one estimated CP component. This structure is attractive from an implementation perspective since it enables parallel processing after the multi-linear decomposition stage. Each branch includes two SVDs, one for ESPRIT and one for component refinement, followed by an ACD procedure. The cost of the two SVDs is dominated by the largest tensor dimension, which yields a combined complexity of Opmax tNC, N S, N Ru3q. The ACD step can be implemented using the exact line search strategy from [20], which requires two eigenvalue decompositions (EVDs) per iteration, with complexity Opmax tNT, N Su3q for the digital case and Opmax tNT, N Cu3q for the hybrid combiner case. Overall, the computational cost is dominated by the CP decomposition stage, so the total complexity of the proposed algorithm scales as Op ˆL3pNC ` NS ` NRq3q. Our analysis corroborates the design objective of the proposed tensor PCE method: to concentrate the main computational effort in a structured tensor factorization step and then exploit lower-dimensional refinements and parallelizable updates to keep the remaining stages manageable.

VI. N UMERICAL RESULTS 

We now compare the proposed CREL-CP algorithm with two representative state-of-the-art (SOTA) baselines: the mul-tiple start SAGE (MS-SAGE) algorithm from [25] and the CP time-varying and frequency-selective (CP-TVFS) method from [16]. The MS-SAGE algorithm can be viewed as a robust extension of the standard SAGE procedure, in which the initialization is strengthened through a multiple-start heuristic. For the tensor decompositions required by both CREL-CP and CP-TVFS, we use Tensorlab 3.0 [27], with GN-DL and alternating least squares (ALS) employed for CREL-CP and CP-TVFS, respectively. The Monte Carlo (MC) setup considers NT “ NR “ 16 

antennas partitioned into D “ 4 subpanels of four elements each. For the CREL-CP and MS-SAGE algorithms, each Tx and Rx RF chain uses discrete Fourier transform (DFT)-based combiners for each subpanel, and D orthogonal pilot streams are transmitted over n. Since CP-TVFS only supports single-stream pilots, an isotropic [20] single-stream pilot is adopted for that method. In addition, CP-TVFS performs numerical line search with a resolution of 1000 points. The pilot frame contains NS “ 64 symbols and NC “ 31 subcarriers. The channel consists of L “ 10 paths, whose parameters

The proposed CREL-CP method exhibits slightly lower accuracy but remains clearly more reliable than CP-TVFS, whose estimates are often severely degraded. The results show that CREL-CP and CP-TVFS are roughly one order of mag-nitude faster than MS-SAGE (the parallel implementation of MS-SAGE considers parallel computations of the path initializations), which confirms the computational advantage of tensor-based processing over more demanding ML-type iterative approaches. Curi-ously, the performance gain from parallelization is apparently not substantial enough to justify its use in practical systems. VII. C ONCLUSION 

In this paper, we introduced the CREL-CP algorithm, a tensor-based PCE method that combines CP decomposition, component refinement, and efficient parameter updates to address fast time-varying channels. The proposed approach improves upon a related tensor-based SOTA baseline while remaining significantly less computationally demanding than a SAGE-based SOTA solution. Although the latter still provides slightly more accurate estimates, CREL-CP achieves a much more attractive complexity-performance tradeoff, with approx-imately one order of magnitude lower execution time. Our results indicate that CREL-CP constitutes a fast and effective alternative to more expensive EM-based PCE algorithms in high-mobility scenarios. REFERENCES [1] H. Chen, F. Ahmad, S.
